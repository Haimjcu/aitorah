What does the Talmud say about honesty in business dealings?
א

The Talmud addresses business honesty extensively, with the foundational principle being emet (truth) as a core value in all commercial dealings.

In Bava Metzia 49a, the Talmud establishes that verbal commitments in commerce carry moral weight — "one who goes back on his word, even if not legally binding, is considered morally deficient." This creates a standard higher than what secular contract law requires.

The Chafetz Chaim in his commentary extends this to prohibit any misleading impression, even through technically true statements — what we today call "half-truths." The concept of geneivat da'at (literally "stealing the mind") covers deception that doesn't involve lying outright.
